CHAPTER 14: FLASH TALKS!

If the sound of the 30-second warning bell is what really gets your neurons firing, then add your name to the GSL, delegate. This committee's agenda: Flash Talks on your research, the Anvesha edition.


In this felonious crossover episode of Shark Tank, the students of IISER Thiruvananthapuram will be presenting before the panel, an insight into any research of their choice. 5 minutes (millenial attention span-friendly, please). 3 slides. 2 minutes for questions.


The challenge is to distil their work to its core and present science that experts and amateurs can understand alike. Participants will be judged on their understanding of the topic, creativity, presentation skills, and how informative their presentation is.

The Anvesha Committee is beyond thrilled to introduce, for the very first time, Flash Talks, open to all students. Join us in a stimulating academic potluck, and serve us your creative rendition of a topic that interests you, whether it's a part of your thesis, an interesting internship, or even research conducted by other scientists from any field of STEM.

Rules:


  1. The presentation should be a maximum of 5 minutes with 2 minutes for questions and comments and comprise a maximum of 3 slides.

  2. Participants must submit a title and short abstract (max. 100 words)  prior to the talk for the judgeâ€™s reference.

  3. The topic of the presentation must be research-oriented.

  4. The work presented can be either work done by/observed by the student in their own research experience or be based on current research happening in their field of interest.

  5. Any data presented from labs that students have worked in must be done after obtaining prior approval from the Principal Investigator of that project.

  6. Any data from published research must be appropriately cited.

  7. Falsification of data is strictly prohibited.

  8. Judgement will not be based on the experiments that are done by students but rather will be on the research that goes behind the presentation.

  9. Credit must be given to all people involved in the research presented, including but not limited to PhD supervisors, Group Leaders and fellow research students.

  10. Presentation of a single research paper will not be counted as a valid entry.

  11. Students are required to verify the origins of their data and verify the permission to present unpublished data prior to the competition. 

  12. Any profanity, propaganda and discriminatory content will lead to immediate disqualification.
