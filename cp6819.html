ESI x SeasonWatch x Anvesha


Are you plant-blind? Even a lifetime may not be enough to get to know plants (nor their names) completely. But we must recognise the significant roles plants play in our lives, and this year, ESI, the Ecological System of IISER Thiruvananthapuram, is here with some events where you can spend quality time with the plants to get to know them better. 


Both of these events will be conducted by SeasonWatch, an India-wide citizen science project led by Nature Conservation Foundation, following the patterns of emergence and maturation of 

leaves, flowers and fruits (“phenology”) on common tree species of India.


If you're curious to know more about SeasonWatch projects or just about Nature Conservation Foundation in general, you can catch up with the SeasonWatch team at a stall during the expo (20th October) itself!

TREE WALK


Do you walk around your lush green campus and think about the greenery you see? Do you wonder what these trees or bushes or herbs are? Let’s find answers to some of these. Let’s go for a tree walk! Tree walk is dedicated a couple of hours to looking closely at the plants around you and learning a thing or two – their names, their peculiarities, maybe their uses and most importantly their awesomeness!


This walk will be led by the SeasonWatch team. We will explore the flora on the campus and discover different ways we connect with the natural world around us. And hopefully, this connection will be sustained for a lifetime.

 
Happy Tree-watching!

The walk will be happening on October 21st, Saturday.

WHAT THE PLANT?  



Do you think you can’t distinguish one plant from another? Our minds register the details of the plants around us. To identify a plant, we only need to compartmentalize these details and use specific terms to describe them. Do this for plants known to you and when you meet a new plant, you can make a comparison and viola! You have identified the plant.


SeasonWatch brings to you What The Plant, a basic plant identification workshop. Herein, we will
-Look at plants known to you and new,
-Learn some ways of looking at plants to register plant details,
-Understand some common plant families
-Use some tools for plant ID
-And also put your plant ID skills to some scientific research.


So let your curiosity for plants bring you to this workshop and we shall break down identifying a plant!

The workshop will be happening on October 22nd, Sunday.
